# Executive summary
This performance report evaluates the sales, volume, and size-level demand metrics captured in the Amazon Sales Report Dashboard. With a total generated revenue of $78,592,538 across 116,649 units sold, the business demonstrates a robust market footprint. However, a critical operational bottleneck is highlighted by an Average Quantity per order of 0.90, indicating that returns, cancellations, or single-item transactions are limiting overall order value.

To maximize profitability, strategic focus must shift toward maximizing high-margin categories, optimizing inventory levels to match active size demand, and implementing cross-selling structures to raise the average basket size.

# Key Insights & Observations
## 1. High-Performing Categories vs. Average Revenue Per Unit
"Set" is the clear market leader, bringing in $39.2M$ in revenue from $45,289$ units. This equates to an average unit price of approximately $865.57$.

"Kurta" is a very close second in terms of volume ($45,045$ units) but generates significantly less revenue ($21.3M$), with an average unit price of about $472.85$.

"Western Dress" is a strong high-value category. Though it only moves $13,943$ units, it generates $11.2M$ in revenue, yielding a high average unit price of $804.53$.

Categories like Bottom, Saree, and Dupatta have negligible contributions to both volume and revenue.

## 2. Size Demand Distribution
The market is heavily concentrated around standard sizing: M ($13.9M$), L ($13.2M$), and XL ($12.4M$) represent the peak of your demand curve.
There is a steep drop-off when moving into extreme plus sizes (4XL, 5XL, 6XL) and "Free" sizes, which collectively generate very little revenue.

# Strategic Recommendations
## 1. Optimize Inventory and Capital Allocation
Action: Pivot stock investment away from slow-moving categories (Dupatta, Saree, Bottom, Blouse) and reallocate that budget to Sets and Kurtas.
Why: "Set" and "Kurta" represent over 77% of all units sold ($90,334$ out of $116,649$). Ensuring these are never out of stock is critical for maintaining cash flow.
## 2. Implement Bundling to Boost the "Average Quantity" Metric
Action: Your average quantity per order is 0.90, which is critically low. Implement "Frequently Bought Together" bundles or multi-pack offers.
Why: Since "Kurta" and "Western Dress" are popular standalone items, try bundling them with lower-performing "Bottoms" or "Dupattas" at a slight discount. This clears out dead stock while driving your average items-per-order above $1.0$.
## 3. Fine-Tune Sizing Runs (Production Matching Demand)
Action: Adjust manufacturing or procurement ratios to match the visual bell curve of your size chart.
Why: M, L, and XL represent the bulk of your revenue. Plus sizes from 4XL to 6XL represent less than 2% of sales. Reducing production of these outlier sizes will prevent capital from being locked up in slow-moving warehouse inventory.
## 4. Capitalize on "Western Dress" High Margins
Action: Increase marketing spend or run targeted promotional campaigns specifically for the Western Dress category.
Why: With an average unit price of over $800$, Western Dresses are highly lucrative. Even a moderate 10% increase in volume for this category will yield a much higher revenue spike compared to a similar volume increase in low-margin Kurtas.
